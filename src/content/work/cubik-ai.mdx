<Block label="01 / Problem">
  <h2>One designer, one month, regulated lending. The unit economics question.</h2>
  <p>
    A founder building Cubik AI needed a working broker-facing platform — not a prototype, not a pitch deck. The brief: indicative quotes for Bridging and Commercial BTL in under a minute, with a defensible audit trail behind every recommendation. <strong>One month. Solo. End-to-end.</strong>
  </p>
  <p>
    The interesting constraint wasn't the timeline. It was the <em>combination</em> — solo builder, regulated lending domain, AI-native tooling, production-ready output. <strong>Can a single designer with the right tooling and the right domain knowledge hold ground that previously needed a team of five?</strong> The case study is the answer.
  </p>
</Block>

<Block label="02 / Approach">
  <h2>Double Diamond, weighted toward build-and-ship.</h2>
  <p>
    The methodology stayed constant; the phase weighting shifted. Discover and Define were compressed by domain expertise carried forward from MFS. Develop and Deliver expanded to absorb the build pipeline.
  </p>
  <PhaseRow
    phases={[

  <InsightGrid
    slot="full"
    insights={[
      {
        num: "Insight 01",
        title: "Compression is a senior judgement, not a shortcut",
        body: "The Bridging and Commercial BTL domain was already mapped from prior fintech work. Repeating discovery would have added weeks for no new information. Knowing not to research what's already known is the senior call."
      },
      {
        num: "Insight 02",
        title: "AI tooling amplifies what the designer brings",
        body: "Claude Code generates working production code in seconds. Whether that code is right depends entirely on whether the designer can specify the correct lending logic, audit obligations, and broker workflow. Domain expertise is the unfair advantage AI tooling amplifies."
      },
      {
        num: "Insight 03",
        title: "Production-ready means audit-ready, from day one",
        body: "Specialist lending isn't a UI problem. Every recommendation needs a defensible decision path. Designing the audit trail at the same time as the front-end was the only way the build was going to ship in a month."
      }
    ]}
  />
</Block>
